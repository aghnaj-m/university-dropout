Student Dropout Trajectories

Visualizing Academic Failure: Pathways to Dropout

Research Question: How do academic performance trajectories (passing vs. failing) in the first and second semesters influence the final outcome (Dropout vs. Enrollment) across different study majors?

The visualization below (Sunburst Chart) traces the flow of students from their chosen Major (Center), through their First and Second Semester performance (Middle Rings), to their Final Status (Outer Ring).

Visual Encoding & Design Choices

To represent this hierarchical data, the following visual variables were mapped:

  • Arc Length (Angle): Represents the Quantity of Students. Larger slices indicate a higher number of students following that specific path.
  • Radial Position (Layers): Represents Time/Progression.
    • Center: Study Major
    • Inner Ring: 1st Semester Result
    • Middle Ring: 2nd Semester Result
    • Outer Ring: Final Outcome
  • Color (Hue): Represents Categorical Status.
    • Green: Academic Success (Passed Semesters).
    • Yellow/Orange: Academic Struggle (Failed Semesters).
    • Red: Negative Outcome (Dropout).
    • Blue: Positive Outcome (Graduate/Enrolled).
  • Adjacency: Represents the Parent-Child Relationship. A student who fails Semester 1 (Parent Node) branches out into either recovering or failing Semester 2 (Child Nodes).
